YouTube to M4A, direct AAC copy

YouTube's native audio stream is AAC inside an .m4a container. When you pick M4A in yt2mp3.lol we skip the re-encoding step and copy YouTube's AAC track straight through — so the output is bit-for-bit identical to what YouTube streams, without any second-generation compression loss.

Why choose M4A over MP3

  • Native on Apple — iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Apple Music all treat M4A as first-class.
  • Smaller at the same perceived quality — AAC is a more modern codec than MP3.
  • Gapless playback — for albums, M4A/AAC supports gapless better than MP3.
  • Metadata and artwork — M4A files carry embedded art reliably across players.

How to convert YouTube to M4A

  1. Paste the URL.
  2. Pick M4A.
  3. Click Convert.
  4. Add the file to iTunes, Apple Music, or drag it into the Files app on iPhone.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the M4A lossless?

It's as lossless as YouTube's original upload — we copy the AAC audio through without re-encoding. The only quality loss is whatever happened during YouTube's original transcode.

What bitrate is the M4A?

Matches the YouTube source — typically 128 kbps AAC for music, sometimes up to 256 kbps for premium content.

Will it play in iTunes?

Yes — M4A is the native iTunes format.

Can I set custom metadata?

We populate title, artist, and album from the YouTube title. You can edit in Mp3tag, Music.app, or any tag editor.
