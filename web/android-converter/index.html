How to Download on Android

1. Open the YouTube app and find your video. 2. Tap the 'Share' button beneath the video and select 'Copy link'. 3. Open Chrome or your preferred browser and navigate to our site. 4. Tap the input box and hit the 'Paste' button. 5. Choose MP3 or MP4 and select your quality. 6. Tap Convert, then tap Download. The file will save to your Downloads folder.

FAQ

Does this work on Samsung Internet and Firefox?

Yes. Works in every modern Android browser.

Do I need storage permission?

No extra permissions needed — the browser handles downloads.

Is this better than downloading an app from the Play Store?

Yes. Google bans legitimate YouTube downloaders from the Play Store. Any app you find there is likely a fake, filled with ads, or a privacy risk. Our web tool is safe, fast, and requires no permissions.

Where do the downloaded files go on my Android?

By default, all Android browsers save files to the 'Downloads' folder on your internal storage. You can access them using the 'Files by Google', 'My Files' (Samsung), or any file manager app.

Will downloaded MP3s automatically show up in my music player?

Yes. Android's Media Storage scanner routinely checks the Downloads folder. Within a few moments of downloading, the MP3 should appear in Samsung Music, Poweramp, Spotify Local, or YouTube Music (Device Files).

Can I share the downloaded video on WhatsApp?

Yes. Once the MP4 is downloaded to your device, you can attach it in WhatsApp, Telegram, or any other messaging app, provided the file size is under the app's sharing limit.

Why did Chrome warn me about downloading a file?

Chrome sometimes shows a generic warning ('File might be harmful') when downloading certain media types, especially if you download many files quickly. Our MP3 and MP4 files are standard media files and are completely safe.

Does the site work on Samsung Internet browser?

Yes, our site is thoroughly tested and fully compatible with Google Chrome, Samsung Internet, Firefox for Android, Brave, and Opera Touch.

How do I change the download location to my SD card?

Open Chrome, tap the three dots (Menu) > Settings > Downloads. Here you can change the download location to your SD Card, or toggle 'Ask where to save files'.

Can I download while on a phone call?

Yes. The conversion happens on our cloud servers, and Chrome handles the file transfer in the background, completely independent of your cellular voice calls.

Do I need to root my Android phone for this?

No root access is required. Our tool uses standard web technologies available on every non-rooted, factory Android device.

Are there annoying pop-up ads on the site?

No. We specifically designed this platform to be clean and ad-free. You will not experience frustrating pop-unders, redirects, or fake 'Download Now' banners.
