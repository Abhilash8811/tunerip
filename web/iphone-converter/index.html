How to Use on iPhone

1. Open the YouTube app, find a video, tap 'Share', and select 'Copy Link'. 2. Swipe up to go home and open Safari. 3. Navigate to our site and paste the link. 4. Choose your format (MP4 for video, M4A/MP3 for audio) and hit Convert. 5. Tap 'Download'. When prompted by Safari, tap 'Download' again. 6. Tap the blue down-arrow in Safari's address bar to access your file.

FAQ

Do I need a jailbreak?

No. Everything happens in the browser on a stock iPhone.

Can I automate this with Shortcuts?

Yes — build a Shortcut that takes a shared URL and opens the converter with it pre-filled.

Do I need to jailbreak my iPhone to use this?

No, absolutely not. Our tool works natively through the standard Safari browser included on every factory iPhone. No jailbreak or third-party profiles are required.

Can I add downloaded MP3s directly to the Apple Music app on my iPhone?

Apple restricts direct imports into the native Apple Music app library from the iOS file system. You can play them in the Files app, or use a free app like VLC. To get them into Apple Music, you still need to sync them via a Mac or PC once.

Why did Safari ask for permission to download?

Since iOS 13, Safari has a built-in download manager. It prompts you to confirm 'Do you want to download [filename]?' as a security measure. Simply tap Download to confirm.

Where do my downloads go?

By default, Safari saves files to the 'Downloads' folder inside your iCloud Drive. You can find this by opening the blue 'Files' app on your iPhone. You can change this to 'On My iPhone' in Settings > Safari > Downloads.

How do I save a downloaded MP4 video to my Photos app?

Open the Files app, tap the downloaded video to open it, tap the Share icon (square with an arrow pointing up) in the bottom corner, and tap 'Save Video'.

Does this work on iPad?

Yes, the experience on iPadOS is identical, taking full advantage of the larger screen and the desktop-class Safari browser.

Can I download while on cellular data (4G/5G)?

Yes, but be mindful of your data cap. Downloading a 1080p MP4 movie can use hundreds of megabytes of data. Audio files (MP3/M4A) use very little data.

Is the site safe from pop-ups and redirects?

Yes. We have built a clean, premium interface. We do not use malicious pop-unders, fake 'update your iPhone' alerts, or spammy redirects common on other converter sites.

Why did the download fail when my screen locked?

If you are downloading a very large file, iOS might suspend Safari's background network activity to save battery. It's best to keep the screen awake during the final file transfer phase.

Can I use Chrome on iOS instead of Safari?

Yes, you can use Google Chrome or Firefox on iOS. They also have built-in download managers that will save the files to your device in a similar manner.
